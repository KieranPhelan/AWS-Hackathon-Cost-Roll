Annual Global Cost Roll process review
Author: - Chris Arundale, Global Finance (Operations)
Date: - 12th Feb 2026 					File: - Annual cost roll process 12_2_26.docx
Purpose: - to review the annual global cost roll process & identify where improved automation can be introduced to reduce the overall processing time, data collection sets & the possibility of human error.
Initial steps: - To determine how automation can proceed, the current process with key steps are shown below.
1)	Weekly data reviews
a.	Identify cost roll errors in the manufacturing & service plants (this is an automated process which runs every Tuesday night for the Wednesday review)
b.	Identify active materials in the manufacturing & service plants where standards have not been posted in the current fiscal year (FY).
2)	Cost roll errors & costs for active materials MUST be resolved before 1st August of every FY. This ensures clean data whilst Legal & Group valuations are posted in August.
3)	Issue Change freeze document to prevent data creation or change to master data which will impact the cost roll process
4)	Review SPK routes to ensure that all pathways are logical & open. 
5)	Load new STO uplift values to support stock movements (VK12, MEK2 condition records)
6)	Set new currency conversion rates for the new FY (Planning rate required for global cost roll)
7)	Extract global cost data for all Proc type F materials which includes PIR inc’g scales, current standard or LIP (last invoice price). This is a lengthy process requiring 2 weeks to extract (all materials) & prepare the data as follows: -
a.	6 SQVI extracts with data from MARA, MARC & MAKT			
i.	1 extract from MAKT for material description
ii.	1 extract from MARA for X plant blocks
iii.	MARC for plant specific data inc’g current std, PP2 & 3, procurement type & plant blocks
1.	1 extract for DE
2.	1 extract for FR, GB & MY
3.	1 extract for US45, US65 & US95
4.	1 extract for Service hubs etc
b.	SQVI extract LIP data: -
i.	One per manufacturing plant except where GB30 & GB45 which can be combined into one extract (due to small volume)
ii.	Sales & Service in 1 extract
c.	SQVI vendor info to translate from Vendor # to Vendor name
d.	SQVI PIR data is extracted in 2 different sets due to FR & US not being correctly maintained!
i.	PIR1 by every plant inc’g FR & US
ii.	PIR2 for FR & US
e.	Scaling info is required from the PIR’s & this is obtained from an Every Angle report created by Sourcing & includes global consumption although not required for ‘cost roll’.
f.	Data from a through to e is imported into Power BI & subsequently exported into Excel by plant where Excel contains logic to recommend recommendations for MM planned price 2 & 3 
g.	The above is generally started at the beginning of July & after approx. 2 weeks is sent to stage 8) immediately below.
8)	Send F type analysis to Plant Financial Controllers & Sourcing Teams for review & new recommendations / agreement.
9)	Review replies from 8) & create upload files by plant defining new MM PP2 & 3. Send files to MDM
10)	MDM use Evolve to upload new values for PP2 & 3 along with the applicability date (for global cost roll this is 31/07/xx & is subsequently amended to 01/08/xx via MM17)
a.	Just a side note – once we move into August the weekly costing runs become defunct due to date restrictions)
11)	Set up multiple costing runs in each of the 23 plants to cover the posting of Legal & Group valuations (Currently 142 costing runs). Just as a side note, the costing runs have master data logic built into them to define what materials to process.
12)	Extract routing data from SAP for each manufacturing plant & present to the business to embody productivity improvements
13)	Upload routing changes – the master data teams process into SAP for each manufacturing plant
14)	From the 2nd week in August the costing runs are processed for all materials except NV’s & RW’s
a.	Where materials are exchanged between manufacturing plants an iterative process is required to ensure final costs are captured. Eg IS600 swabs are supplied by MY & go into IS600 FG build by Hemel. The resetting of intercompany costs through this iterative process is forwarded to MDM to upload via Evolve
15)	The process of calculating costs for NV (defective materials) is based on 3% of the new material in the source plant. RW (repaired parts) materials can either be reworked in-house or at an external vendor. Many of the processes mentioned above are further invoked to complete the posting of legal & group valuation for these materials
16)	Prior to any costs being posted data is extracted from the costing runs & an impact analysis conducted on stock valuation. If change is significant, then authorisation is sort from a Financial director to explain & post the costs if acceptable. If not acceptable then further cost analysis is conducted.
17)	All analysis files are extracted via Excel & retained as an auditable reference.
18)	Some plants sit outside of SAP & the cost changes are communicated to the relevant controllers
19)	Changes in spares & saleable materials are notified to the Sales teams to potentially update condition record ZPRI to reflect revised selling prices
20)	September is often devoted to receiving & replying to queries from many plants to substantiate the cost changes & potential impact on revenue.
21)	Once the August cost roll is complete, set up new costing runs to monitor & correct errors throughout the year. 

Tools currently used during the process
a)	Power BI
b)	Excel
c)	Evolve
d)	SAP
Next steps: -
i)	Provide more detail around each of the above key steps
ii)	Possibly show the potential material movements ie route to market – could use my Morpho material movements documented around 2019
iii)	Take a sample FG (eg IS600) & along with individual data extracts map the cost movements & confirmations. 
iv)	Identify all error types from the weekly costing runs & define solutions for each
v)	Should we challenge the SPK routes by analysing the actual routes used during the FY to confirm if they are logical & adjust as appropriate
vi)	Its also frustrating that Service Supply chain periodically ask for the routes to be changed during the FY – this is frustrating & time consuming – best to complete before the August cost roll
vii)	How to accommodate new plants eg IE30 & IE45
viii)	Also, one massive problem is non-MDM individuals with access to change the master data eg removing material blocks without understanding the full implications. This has been an age-old problem & has never satisfactorily been resolved!

1.0	PURPOSE
To ensure that there is a consistent global approach to create/ change and govern the Material Master Data in SAP.

2.0	SCOPE

This document applies to all Departments and teams who are responsible for the management and governance of the completeness and accuracy of this Data.

3.0	DEFINITION
Maintaining planned price 2 & 3 values is a method to allow a cost roll of a material in order to set a standard cost of a material
Planned Price 2 and 3 is situated in material master data and can be found using transaction code MM03 for viewing purposes.  Maintenance is via MM02 and to create this data is via MM01.  The tab in material master where these data fields sit are in the Costing 2 tab:
 

Planned Price 2 & 3 are in the ‘planned prices’ section of the Costing 2 tab.  When a planned price 2 and 3 value is entered, we always populate a “valid from” date in to the fields ‘planned price date 2 & 3’
 

Planned price 2 with associated date 
This is the Legal price & includes uplift for STO materials  when set in the receiving site – this value is used in the cost roll process & should include a validity date (local currency at Bud XR)

Planned price 3 with associated date
This is the Group price & will not include any uplift – this value is used in the cost roll process & should include a validity date (local currency at Bud XR)


The current standard cost of a material is shown in the ‘standard cost estimate’ section of the Costing 2 tab.  If a value is not shown in the ‘planned price’ highlighted in yellow here, then the material has not formally been cost rolled:
 

There should be no uplift for a material in the same entity - e.g. DE30 & DE45


4.0	RULES AND IMPACT FOR THE BUSINESS

Planned Price 2
Planned Price 2 should be populated for all materials which are externally procured (procurement type F) & includes materials obtained from other SD sites. 
PP2 should not be populated for materials which are procurement type F & sub contract (special procurement code 30). In this instance a sub-contracting Purchase Info record should be generated by the local procurement team
When populated, PP2 should also include the ‘Planned price date 2’ which will be the date the value is added or amended
PP2 will be populated to allow the cost roll activity to post values to the Legal Price or Company Code Currency in Accounting 1
PP2 is in local currency
Where PP2 is maintained for materials procured from other SD sites it will include some mark-up. This value should be obtained from the local finance representative.
PP2 will be used in the cost roll process
PP2 should be maintained annually in preparation for the end of year cost roll.
If a material is procured from an external vendor then PP2 will equal PP3
Planned Price 2 can only be applied to material types which have the Costing 2 tab eg FERT, HALB, ROH, HAWA, HIBE, KMAT, PROD, VERP & FHMI


Planned Price 3
Planned Price 3 should be populated for all materials which are externally procured (procurement type F) & includes materials obtained from other SD sites.
PP3 should not be populated for materials which are procurement type F & sub contract (special procurement code 30). In this instance a sub-contracting Purchase Info record should be generated by the local procurement team
When populated, PP3 should also include the ‘Planned price date 3’ which will be the date the value is added or amended
PP3 will be populated to allow the cost roll activity to post values to the Group Price or Group Valuation in Accounting 1
PP3 is also referred to as the Group Price 
PP3 is in local currency
Where PP3 is maintained for materials procured from other SD sites the value should be derived from the source site & converted into the local currency.
PP3 will be used in the cost roll process
PP3 should be maintained annually in preparation for the end of year cost roll.
If a material is procured from an external vendor then PP2 will equal PP3
Planned Price 3 can only be applied to material types which have the Costing 2 tab eg FERT, HALB, ROH, HAWA, HIBE, KMAT, PROD, VERP & FHMI

3.0	DEFINITION
The cost-roll process is applied in order to set the standard cost/ price of a material or group of materials.
 
SAP transaction (T) codes:-						
a)	‘CK11N’ to ‘create a material cost estimate’
b)	‘CK24’ to ‘mark a standard price’ 
c)	‘CK24’ to ‘release a standard price’ (same T code as ‘b’)
d)	‘MM03’ to view Material Master data
e)	‘ZSCM_BOMECM’ to extract a multi-level BoM (initial user access may be a problem!)
Note:- the following process can be used to cost roll an  individual or group of materials & assumes that all of the costing data has been correctly applied in either Material Master, Purchase Info Records (PIR) or that an existing reliable standard has previously be posted. Error resolution is partially covered on the last page.
As a general note, SAP adopts the following hierarchy when attempting to determine costs – if no costs can be determined, then a red error will be identified using a formal traffic light system.
1.	Material Master, Costing 2 tab, Planned Price 2 (legal) & Planned Price 3 (group) with associated ‘valid from dates’.
2.	PIR
3.	Existing Standard – seen in Material Master, costing 2 tab.

Items to consider when setting a standard cost 

Requests to set a standard are likely to result from:-
a.	 Material extensions to multiple sites completed by GDG. The requests are currently via an e-mail (group or individual)
b.	Site controllers advising that the current standards are completely in error eg material currently valued at $0.01 now cost rolled & posted at $1115.14
c.	New products, spares or service materials loaded to SAP
d.	Annual group cost roll.
Consideration should be given to:-
1.	Is the Material Type correctly set – if not, then STO uplifts will be incorrect.
2.	Is the source of supply correctly set in the receiving plants material master record?
3.	Is the cost correctly ‘posted’ in the plant identified as the source of supply, has it been formally cost rolled or just manually posted – manually posted costs are unacceptable.
4.	Check that the costs posted costs are correct, maybe review PIR’s, MB51 for recent receipts
5.	Check the costing lot size – if greater than 1, this may require further review to set a 1 off cost in the receiving plant
6.	Check the Price Unit – if greater than 1, this may require further review to set a 1 off cost in the receiving plant
7.	Have values been correctly set for Planned Price 2 & 3 in the receiving plants material master data. This will be based on the material type, plant to plant uplift & currency conversion. 
8.	Currency conversion to be completed using the budget rate.
9.	If there is any doubt about the costs in the supplying plant then conduct a CK11N review. If appropriate, ask the site financial controller to post a new value.
10.	Is there stock in either plant – be aware that setting a new standard will result in a re-valuation which may be unacceptable.
11.	Consider when it is appropriate to set a new standard, for example, if a material valuation was generated in period 1 but a massive change in the build programme has resulted in dramatically different costs. This may result in a massive PPV
12.	Costs in the same entity for the same material should be identical eg GB00 = GB30 & GB45, US00 = US45, US65 & US80
13.	An identical STO material in the same entity should indicate the same source of supply using the Special Procurement Key in MM, MRP2. Note, the SPK code will, by necessity, be different.
14.	Problems may be experience around month end when attempting to post standards: either complete before closure or shortly after the new period is opened.

THE GOVERNANCE PROCESS NEEDS TO BE CONFIRMED WITH THE FINANCE TEAM – IF A STANDARD PRICE CHANGES IN ONE MANUFACTURING SITE, HOW IS THIS CHANGE EXTENDED TO THE OTHER SITES.
•	Cost-roll is reviewed annually by the Finance team.
•	Individual materials are cost-rolled whenever the material is extended to another plant or when a new material is created in the system.
•	Monthly Audit reports are run by GDG to monitor all newly extended materials have been cost-rolled.

1 Overview
1.1 Introduction
The manual has been written to define the purpose of used fields in SAP Material Master for Smiths
Detection. The information provided here is written with the GB plants as the primary focus. An
addendum has been provided at the end of each tab, to provide specific details for each plant. This
includes Wiesbaden (DE), Vitry (FR), Malaysia (MY) and America (US).
Understanding the purpose of the fields in SAP Material Master is the foundation that allows key
business activities such as material creation or extension to be accurately and efficiently completed.
The intention of this manual is to provide that solid foundation on which users within Smiths
Detection can use when they require advice or guidance on the field usage in SAP to complete one
of these key activities.
NB:
This document was written when using SAP ECC in Classic View for the screenshots. Other views
may cause difficulties for the end user working with the information provided.
1

The information in this manual was collected over the course of financial year 2025 – 2026. The

details have been gathered using multiple resources. First and foremost, it has lent on the

Plant
• GB
o A Plant is a location where Materials are produced, purchased, stored, inventoried,
shipped, received, and valuated, or from which Services are provided.
o A Plant can only be assigned to one Sales Organisation.
o The Plant is the only level where Material Standard Cost can be defined.
o Processes where the Plant is involved include:
▪ Material costing and valuation
▪ Materials resource planning and shipping/receiving of Materials
▪ Transactions involving Materials (e.g. production orders, sales orders,
purchase orders, etc.)
o There is no assignment between sales organisation and plant level of material
master.

Item 209 – Lot size
• Determines the method for calculating the replenishment quantity that will be used by MRP.
• Required if MRP type is MRP relevant.

requirement (e.g. requirement for 4 MRP created a replenishment of 4 pieces).

month’s requirement.

requirement.

week’s requirement.

meets that quarter’s requirement.

20.6 In-House Production Time In Days Section
This section specifies the time in workdays needed to produce the material in-house.
• Can be maintained either Lot-size dependent or lot-size independent times.
• Lot-size dependent times can:
o Be entered into the ‘Lot Size Dependent’ fields directly or
o Can be populated using a separate transaction CA97 which uses the times from the
material’s routing.
• Lot-size independent time does not take into account Production Order quantity when
scheduling the production order.
o Example: if time is entered as 4 days, then an order of 1 unit or 200 units will be
calculated as 4 days for both.
• When using lot size dependent fields then planned order quantity is considered when the
order is scheduled.
303 304 305 306 233

Item 303 – Setup time
• Identifies the labour time to ‘setup’ a job activity -a one-off cost per production order (data
from routing). Also includes ‘Machine’ time which is non costed & a dwell, for example,
when a material is held in the wash & bake cycle.
Item 304 – Processing time
• Identifies the elapsed (not attended) labour time to complete manufacture specified in a
Production order (data comes from the routing & is the attended time divided by crew size).
Item 305 – Interoperation
• Specifies the number of workdays required for the transition of a produced material
between work centers. The interoperation time is required to calculate the lot size
dependent in-house production time. The system then uses the in-house production time to
determine basic dates for planned orders in materials planning.
• The default value in GB is 1.

Appendix B – How to Build a Product Hierarchy Number
The product hierarchy number is built up in four stages. The following example is applicable for a
related Ionscan 600 product:
